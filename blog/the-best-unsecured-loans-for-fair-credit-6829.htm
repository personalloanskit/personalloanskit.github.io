The Best Unsecured Loans for Fair Credit

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Navigating the world of personal finance can be challenging, especially if you have fair credit. With rising inflation, economic uncertainty, and unexpected expenses, many Americans are looking for flexible borrowing options. Unsecured loans—those that don’t require collateral—can be a lifeline, but finding the best ones for fair credit (typically FICO scores between 580 and 669) requires careful research.

Why Unsecured Loans Matter for Fair Credit Borrowers

Unsecured loans are popular because they don’t put your assets at risk. Unlike secured loans (e.g., auto or home equity loans), you won’t lose your car or house if you default. However, lenders see fair-credit borrowers as higher risk, which means higher interest rates and stricter approval criteria.

Key Benefits of Unsecured Loans

  • No collateral required – Your personal assets aren’t tied to the loan.
  • Fast funding – Many lenders deposit funds within 1-3 business days.
  • Flexible use – Cover medical bills, debt consolidation, home repairs, or emergencies.

Top Lenders Offering Unsecured Loans for Fair Credit

1. Upgrade

Upgrade is a standout for fair-credit borrowers, offering loans from $1,000 to $50,000 with APRs starting at 8.49%. They also provide credit monitoring tools to help improve your score.

Pros:
- Accepts credit scores as low as 580
- Fast approval and funding
- No prepayment penalties

Cons:
- Origination fees (1.85% - 9.99%)
- Higher APRs for lower credit tiers

2. Avant

Avant specializes in borrowers with fair to poor credit, with loan amounts ranging from $2,000 to $35,000. Their APRs range from 9.95% to 35.99%.

Pros:
- Soft credit check for prequalification
- Funds available as soon as the next business day
- Mobile app for easy account management

Cons:
- High maximum APR
- Administrative fee up to 4.75%

3. LendingPoint

LendingPoint focuses on near-prime borrowers (scores 600+) and offers loans from $2,000 to $36,500. APRs range from 7.99% to 35.99%.

Pros:
- Next-day funding available
- No prepayment penalties
- Transparent fee structure

Cons:
- Not available in Nevada or West Virginia
- Origination fees up to 7%

How to Improve Your Chances of Approval

Check Your Credit Report First

Errors on your credit report can drag down your score. Use AnnualCreditReport.com to review your reports from all three bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) for free.

Reduce Your Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)

Lenders prefer a DTI below 36%. Pay down existing debt or increase your income to improve your odds.

Consider a Co-Signer

If possible, adding a co-signer with good credit can help you secure better rates.

Alternatives to Unsecured Loans

Credit Unions

Local credit unions often offer lower rates than big banks, especially if you’re a member.

Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Lending

Platforms like Prosper and Upstart connect borrowers with individual investors, sometimes offering better terms for fair credit.

Credit-Builder Loans

If you’re rebuilding credit, a credit-builder loan (like those from Self) can help boost your score over time.

The Impact of Rising Interest Rates

With the Federal Reserve increasing rates to combat inflation, borrowing costs have surged. Fair-credit borrowers are especially affected, as lenders offset risk with higher APRs. Shopping around for the best rates is more important than ever.

Final Tips Before Applying

  • Compare multiple lenders – Use prequalification tools to see estimated rates without hurting your credit.
  • Read the fine print – Watch for hidden fees, prepayment penalties, and variable rates.
  • Borrow only what you need – Avoid overextending yourself financially.

By understanding your options and improving your credit health, you can find an unsecured loan that fits your needs—even with fair credit.
