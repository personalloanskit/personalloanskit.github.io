Fig Loans: How They Compare to Installment Loans

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

When it comes to borrowing money, consumers today have more options than ever before. From traditional installment loans to innovative alternatives like Fig Loans, the lending landscape is evolving rapidly. But how do these options stack up against each other? And which one is the right choice for your financial needs?

Understanding Fig Loans

What Are Fig Loans?

Fig Loans is a fintech company that offers small-dollar loans designed to help borrowers with limited credit history or lower credit scores. Unlike traditional lenders, Fig Loans focuses on financial inclusion, providing affordable and transparent loan products. Their mission is to break the cycle of predatory lending by offering lower interest rates and flexible repayment terms.

Key Features of Fig Loans

  • Small Loan Amounts: Typically ranging from $300 to $750.
  • Lower APRs: Compared to payday loans, Fig Loans offer significantly lower annual percentage rates.
  • Credit Building: Payments are reported to credit bureaus, helping borrowers improve their credit scores.
  • No Hidden Fees: Transparent pricing with no prepayment penalties.

Traditional Installment Loans

How Do Installment Loans Work?

Installment loans are a common borrowing option where you receive a lump sum upfront and repay it over a fixed period in regular installments. These loans can be secured (backed by collateral) or unsecured (based on creditworthiness).

Pros and Cons of Installment Loans

Advantages

  • Higher Loan Amounts: Often available in amounts from $1,000 to $50,000 or more.
  • Fixed Repayment Schedule: Predictable monthly payments make budgeting easier.
  • Versatility: Can be used for various purposes, such as debt consolidation, home improvements, or medical expenses.

Disadvantages

  • Stricter Eligibility: Often require good to excellent credit scores.
  • Longer Approval Times: Traditional banks may take days or weeks to process applications.
  • Potential for High Interest: If you have poor credit, rates can be steep.

Fig Loans vs. Installment Loans: A Side-by-Side Comparison

Loan Amounts

  • Fig Loans: Best for small, short-term needs ($300–$750).
  • Installment Loans: Better suited for larger expenses ($1,000+).

Interest Rates and Fees

  • Fig Loans: APRs are capped to prevent predatory lending, often under 36%.
  • Installment Loans: Rates vary widely—some as low as 5% for prime borrowers, but subprime borrowers may face APRs exceeding 100%.

Credit Requirements

  • Fig Loans: Designed for borrowers with poor or no credit history.
  • Installment Loans: Typically require a minimum credit score of 580–600 for approval.

Speed of Funding

  • Fig Loans: Fast approval, often within one business day.
  • Installment Loans: May take several days to weeks, especially with traditional banks.

Impact on Credit Score

  • Fig Loans: Reports payments to credit bureaus, helping build credit.
  • Installment Loans: Also reports to credit bureaus, but missed payments can hurt your score.

Why Fig Loans Are Gaining Popularity

Addressing the Payday Loan Crisis

Many borrowers trapped in the payday loan cycle face exorbitant interest rates (often 400% APR or higher). Fig Loans provides a more ethical alternative with capped rates and no rollover traps.

Financial Inclusion

Traditional lenders often exclude low-income or credit-challenged individuals. Fig Loans fills this gap by offering accessible credit without exploiting borrowers.

The Rise of Fintech Solutions

As digital banking grows, consumers are embracing fintech lenders like Fig Loans for their convenience, transparency, and customer-centric approach.

When to Choose Fig Loans Over Installment Loans

  • You need a small amount quickly.
  • You have bad credit or no credit history.
  • You want to avoid high-interest debt traps.

When Installment Loans Might Be Better

  • You need a larger sum for a major expense.
  • You have good credit and qualify for low rates.
  • You prefer longer repayment terms (12–84 months).

The Future of Borrowing

With increasing financial inequality and rising living costs, fair lending solutions like Fig Loans are becoming essential. Meanwhile, traditional installment loans remain a viable option for those with strong credit. The key is understanding your needs and choosing the right product wisely.

As the lending industry evolves, consumers must stay informed to make the best financial decisions—whether that means turning to innovative fintech solutions or sticking with conventional loan structures.
